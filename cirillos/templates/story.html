The Story of Cirillo's

& Bob Mersereau

  • Leaving a legacy.

    Cirillos was born from my love of cooking and family. My father, Robert Mersereau Sr., was born in 1942 and grew up in Catholic orphanages in Philadelphia. At the age of 12 he was adopted by the Mersereau Family and grew up in and around the Philadelphia area. Curious about who his real mother was and her story, he searched for her all his life and finally found her to only learn she had sadly passed away only six months prior. Her name was Josephine Cirillo.

    Photo of
    Josephine

  • West Chester is home.

    To honor Josephine and my father, I opened an artisan pizza restaurant where family is celebrated daily! On the corner of West Market and New Street in the Borough of West Chester sits Cirillo's Artisan Pizza where you can catch Josephine’s great grandsons and I waiting on folks with a smile. I grew up in West Chester and love this town as it feels like home to me. I attended St. Agnes Elementary School and Bishop Shanahan High School when it was in the borough.

    Photo of
    Cirillo's

  • Why dough?

    For as long as I remember I loved making pizza, especially for my family. This passion has led me to change direction at this location and return to my roots making Artisan pizzas by hand. Creating layers of flavor that comes from a mix of unique fresh ingredients is my passion and I am excited to share it with you! So stop in, there is always something cooking and we’re here waiting for you and your family!
